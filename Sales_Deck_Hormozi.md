# CourtFlow — Sales Deck Content
## 8 Slides | Alex Hormozi Grand Slam Offer Framework

**Audience:** Venue owners, facility managers, recreation directors, franchise operators
**Goal:** Convert B2B prospects into trial sign-ups or demo bookings
**Framework:** Hormozi Value Equation — Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

---

## SLIDE 1 — THE PAINFUL TRUTH (Problem Agitation)

### "You're Losing $2,400/month Per Court in Dead Time"

**Visual:** A photo-realistic depiction of an empty pickleball court with a clipboard hanging on the net post, overlaid with a ticking clock graphic.

**Content:**

> Right now, at your facility:
>
> - **Courts sit empty 15–20 minutes between games.** That's 3+ hours of wasted court time per session.
> - **Your best players stop coming** because they waited 45 minutes and played once.
> - **Staff spend more time managing rotations** than helping players.
> - **You have zero data** on who's playing, how often, or whether they'll come back.
>
> This isn't a pickleball problem. It's a **revenue and retention problem.**

**Speaker Notes (Hormozi principle — Agitate the pain):**
Don't sell the solution yet. Let them sit with the cost of inaction. The pain of the current state must be visceral. Frame the problem in dollars, not minutes. Every venue owner understands lost revenue. The $2,400 figure comes from: 4 courts × 15 min idle × 8 rotations/session × 20 sessions/month × $1/min court value. Adjust for your market.

### Delivery Tips (Hormozi-style)

1. **Don't pitch features. Pitch the transformation.** Nobody buys court management software. They buy "every court full, every player happy, staff in control."

2. **Use their language.** Say "open play night" not "session." Say "rotation" not "queue algorithm." They don't care how it works. They care that it works.

3. **The price should feel like a rounding error.** After showing $5K/mo ROI, $99/mo should barely register. If they're thinking about price, you haven't sold the value.

4. **Let the demo sell.** The best close for CourtFlow is showing it live. 60 seconds of the TV display auto-rotating players is worth more than 30 minutes of slides.

5. **Handle the "we're fine with our current system" objection:** "That's what every venue says until they lose their best players to the venue down the street that installed CourtFlow. You're not competing with us. You're competing with the future of every other facility in your market."

6. **Anchor on pain, not features.** Start every conversation with: "How do you currently handle rotations?" Then listen. Every answer is ammunition for slides 1 and 2.
